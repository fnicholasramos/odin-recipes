Panna Cotta

Panna cotta is a silky, smooth Italian dessert with a creamy, melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract (or 1 vanilla bean)
  • 1 tablespoon of gelatin powder
  • 3 tablespoons of water (for dissolving gelatin)
  • Fresh berries or fruit sauce (for topping, optional)

Steps:

  1. Bloom the Gelatin: In a small bowl, sprinkle 1 tablespoon of gelatin powder over 3 tablespoons of water. Let it sit for about 5 minutes to soften.
  2. Heat the Cream: In a saucepan, heat 2 cups of heavy cream and 1/4 cup of sugar over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves, but don‚Äôt let it boil.
  3. Add Vanilla: Stir in 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract (or scrape the seeds of 1 vanilla bean) into the cream mixture.
  4. Dissolve the Gelatin: Remove the cream from the heat and stir in the softened gelatin until fully dissolved.
  5. Pour and Chill: Pour the mixture into small cups or ramekins. Let them cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or until fully set.
  6. Serve: Serve chilled, and top with fresh berries or fruit sauce if desired.
